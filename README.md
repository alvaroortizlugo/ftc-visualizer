# Interactive FTC Visualizer

An interactive visualization of the **Fundamental Theorem of Calculus**, built as a teaching artifact for the *AI Literacy, Research & Teaching* workshop at the University of Cincinnati.

## The point

The FTC says: **F′(x) = f(x)**.

Translated into plain language: **the slope of the total accumulation at any point equals the height of the rate at that same point.** This visualizer makes that equality visible. Drag your cursor across either graph; the numbers in the highlighted panel stay equal no matter where you move it.

## How to use

1. Pick a real-world context from the dropdown: drug absorption, water filling a tank, distance from velocity, or a custom shape.
2. Drag your cursor across either the rate graph (purple) or the total graph (green).
3. Watch the **click moment panel** below the graphs: the height of the rate and the slope of the total are always equal.

## How it was built

This visualization was developed as a worked example for a workshop assignment that asks students to build an interactive FTC tool with AI assistance, then submit:

- The working interactive (this artifact)
- A 500-word design rationale defending their choices
- A 90-second screen recording walking a peer through the tool
- An AI-use reflection documenting prompts that worked and prompts that didn't
